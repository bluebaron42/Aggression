## Key Features Implemented

### 9 Interactive Slides
1. **Title Slide** - Introduction with animated icons
2. **Do Now Quiz** - 5-question retrieval practice with scoring
3. **Neural Map** - Interactive amygdala/OFC visualization
4. **Chemical Balance** - Serotonin/testosterone interactive sliders
5. **Study Briefing** - Gospic et al. (2011) research overview with ultimatum game
6. **fMRI Simulation** - Interactive lab simulation with drug/provocation controls
7. **Evidence** - Research findings cards (Gospic, Virkkunen)
8. **Critical Analysis** - Dual-hormone hypothesis and limitations
9. **Essay Plan** - Exam revision with AO1/AO3 breakdown (16 marks)

## Lesson Content

### Aggression L01: Neural & Hormonal Mechanisms

**Topics:**
- Limbic system (amygdala) and threat response
- Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) and impulse control
- Serotonin as inhibitory neurotransmitter
- Testosterone and social dominance
- Dual-hormone hypothesis (testosterone × cortisol)

**Research Studies Covered:**
- Gospic et al. (2011) - fMRI ultimatum game with benzodiazepines
- Virkkunen et al. (1994) - Serotonin deficiency in offenders
- Dolan et al. (2001) - Testosterone-aggression correlation
- Carre & Mehta (2011) - Dual-hormone hypothesis

## Customization Guide

### Change Colors
Edit `tailwind.config.js` theme colors

### Add New Lessons
1. Add lesson data in `App.jsx` lessons array
2. Create new component files in `src/components/`
3. Add new case in `renderLesson1()` function

### Modify Quiz Questions
Edit `lesson1DoNow` array in `App.jsx`

### Change Slide Content
Edit individual component files directly
